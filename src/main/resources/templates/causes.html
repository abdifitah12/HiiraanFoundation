Causes of Flooding

1. Heavy Rainfall

Prolonged or intense rainfall can saturate the ground, causing rivers and lakes to overflow and resulting in floods.

2. River Overflow

Rivers can overflow due to melting snow, intense rain, or storms, leading to flooding in nearby areas.

3. Urbanization

The expansion of cities reduces natural land for water absorption, leading to increased surface runoff and flooding.

4. Deforestation

Deforestation reduces the ground's ability to absorb water, increasing water runoff and the risk of floods.

5. Poor Drainage Systems

Inadequate drainage systems can lead to water accumulation, especially in urban areas, resulting in flooding.

6. Climate Change

Climate change leads to extreme weather and rising sea levels, both of which increase the risk of flooding.

7. Dams and Levee Failures

Failures of dams or levees can release large volumes of water, causing sudden and severe flooding.

8. Storm Surges and Tidal Effects

Storm surges and high tides push seawater inland, resulting in flooding in coastal areas.
